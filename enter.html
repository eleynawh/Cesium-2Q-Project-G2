Taylor Swift

Taylor Swift’s The Life of a Showgirl is shaking things up—and not everyone is thrilled. Instead of the heart-wrenching storytelling fans know her for, she’s gone bold, raw, and a little in-your-face, with lyrics and themes that feel more performative than personal. Longtime listeners were especially surprised by the fifth track, “Eldest Daughter,” which breaks the tradition of gut-punch ballads they’d come to expect. Still, despite the mixed reactions, the album is impossible to ignore: all 12 songs landed in the top 12 of the Billboard Hot 100, it debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200, and Swift proves once again that even when she divides her fans, she dominates the charts.

D4vd

D4vd, usually celebrated for turning heartbreak into art, is now caught in a real-life mystery that’s unfolding far from the studio. An unreleased love song called Celeste has become an unexpected backdrop to a case involving a woman found dead in his Tesla, a police search of a Hollywood Hills home tied to him, and a private investigator hinting at strange items discovered on the property. He isn’t considered a suspect, and the cause of death remains undetermined, but he’s already brought in a high-profile defense attorney as the situation grows increasingly surreal.
